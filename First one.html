  • Make a plan
  • Specific: Your goal is clearly defined. “I want to make more money,” is vague. “I want to make $10,000 per month,” is specific. Measurable: You need to quantify your goal so you know you achieved it. This is where being specific helps. What constitutes "more" in more money? A specific dollar amount is measurable. Attainable: It’s good to set goals that make you stretch and challenge yourself, but you set yourself up for frustration and failure if your goal is impossible. Relevant: Your goals should fit within your ultimate plans in life. Time: You’ve set a date by which your goal will be achieved.
